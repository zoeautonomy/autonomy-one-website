10 Free ChatGPT Prompts for Insurance Agents

Ready to experience the power of AI-assisted writing? Here are 10 carefully crafted prompts you can copy and use immediately. These prompts are designed to generate professional, customizable content that you can adapt to your specific needs.

Prompt #1: Policy Comparison Explainer

Act as an experienced insurance agent explaining coverage options to a client with no insurance background. Compare [POLICY TYPE A] and [POLICY TYPE B] in simple terms. Include: key differences in coverage, typical cost range differences, scenarios where each option is best, and a clear recommendation framework. Use everyday analogies to explain complex concepts. Keep the tone friendly and educational, not salesy.

Prompt #2: New Client Welcome Email

Write a warm, professional welcome email for a new insurance client who just purchased a [POLICY TYPE] policy. Include: a thank you message, what they can expect in terms of communication, how to reach me for questions, a brief mention of policy documents they'll receive, and an invitation to schedule a review call in 6 months. Keep it under 200 words and make it feel personal, not automated.

Prompt #3: Claims Process Guide

Create a step-by-step guide for a client who needs to file a [CLAIM TYPE] claim. Explain the process in 6-8 clear steps, including what documentation they need to gather, realistic timeline expectations, and how I'll support them throughout. Use a reassuring, empathetic tone since they're likely stressed. End with my contact information and office hours.

Prompt #4: Lead Follow-Up Sequence

Generate a 5-email follow-up sequence for a lead who requested a [POLICY TYPE] quote but hasn't responded. Space the emails over 3 weeks. Each email should: have a unique angle/hook, provide genuine value (not just "checking in"), address a common objection or concern, and include a soft call-to-action. Make emails progressively shorter. Final email should be a respectful "closing the loop" message.

Prompt #5: Social Media Educational Post

Write a LinkedIn post explaining [INSURANCE CONCEPT] for homeowners/business owners/families. Make it engaging with a hook in the first line, use a relatable scenario or story, include 3 key takeaways, and end with a question to encourage comments. Keep it under 150 words. Add appropriate emoji sparingly. Include 3-5 relevant hashtags.

Prompt #6: Renewal Reminder Email

Write a policy renewal reminder email for a client whose [POLICY TYPE] expires in 30 days. Include: acknowledgment of their loyalty, brief summary of any coverage changes or market updates they should know about, option to schedule a review call, and clear next steps to renew. Make it feel like a helpful reminder, not a sales pitch.

Prompt #7: Objection Response Script

Create a conversational response script for when a prospect says "[COMMON OBJECTION]" about purchasing [POLICY TYPE] insurance. Include: acknowledgment of their concern, reframe the perspective, provide relevant data or example, ask a follow-up question to continue the conversation. Keep it natural—not scripted-sounding. Provide 2-3 variations.

Prompt #8: Cross-Sell Introduction

Write an email to an existing [CURRENT POLICY] client introducing them to [ADDITIONAL POLICY TYPE] coverage. Explain how the coverages complement each other, give a specific scenario where the additional coverage would help, mention any multi-policy discount available, and offer a no-pressure review call. Keep the tone helpful rather than pushy.

Prompt #9: Referral Request Message

Create a referral request message for satisfied clients after a successful [CLAIM/POLICY PURCHASE/RENEWAL]. Thank them genuinely, ask if they know anyone who might benefit from similar coverage, make it easy to refer (provide what to say), and mention any referral appreciation program if applicable. Keep it brief and sincere—under 100 words.

Prompt #10: Annual Review Invitation

Write an email inviting a client to their annual policy review meeting. Explain why annual reviews matter (life changes, coverage gaps, potential savings), suggest 2-3 specific discussion points relevant to [POLICY TYPE], offer flexible meeting options (in-person, phone, video), and include a scheduling link placeholder. Make them feel valued, not obligated.

How to Customize These Prompts for Maximum Results

These prompts are designed as starting frameworks, but the real magic happens when you customize them for your specific situation. Here are some tips for getting the best results:

Add context about your client: The more specific information you include—age, family situation, risk factors, previous conversations—the more personalized and relevant the output will be.

Specify your voice and brand: If your agency has a particular tone (formal, friendly, technical, casual), mention this in your prompt. You can even paste examples of your previous writing and ask ChatGPT to match the style.

Iterate and refine: Don't expect perfection on the first try. Use follow-up prompts like "Make this more concise" or "Add more empathy to the opening" to dial in exactly what you need.
