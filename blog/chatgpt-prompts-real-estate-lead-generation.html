Cold Outreach Scripts That Actually Get Responses

Cold outreach is where most agents struggle the most. The challenge isn't making calls or sending emails—it's crafting messages that cut through the noise and compel strangers to respond. Here's where ChatGPT becomes your secret weapon.

Prompt #1: The Neighborhood Expert Cold Call Script

Copy-Paste Prompt
You are an expert real estate copywriter specializing in cold outreach. Create a cold call script for a real estate agent targeting homeowners in [NEIGHBORHOOD NAME]. The script should:

- Open with a value-first statement (not a greeting that screams "sales call")
- Reference a specific recent sale or market trend in the area
- Position the agent as a neighborhood specialist, not just another realtor
- Include a soft call-to-action that offers value (free home valuation, market report, etc.)
- Handle the "I'm not interested" objection with grace
- Keep the total script under 90 seconds when spoken

Market context: [INSERT LOCAL MARKET CONDITIONS - e.g., "homes selling 12% above asking, average 8 days on market"]

Agent's unique value proposition: [INSERT YOUR SPECIALTY - e.g., "specializes in downsizing seniors, has sold 47 homes in this neighborhood"]

Make the script conversational, not salesy. The goal is to start a relationship, not close a deal on the first call.

This prompt works because it forces ChatGPT to create context-specific scripts rather than generic templates. The key is filling in those brackets with real data from your market—that's what separates a script that converts from one that gets hung up on.

Prompt #2: The Email Sequence for Cold Prospects

Copy-Paste Prompt
Create a 5-email nurture sequence for cold real estate prospects who have shown initial interest but haven't responded to follow-up. Each email should:

Email 1: Re-engage with a compelling market insight specific to their neighborhood
Email 2: Share a mini case study of a recent similar sale (without being braggy)
Email 3: Provide genuine value - a downloadable resource or exclusive information
Email 4: Create subtle urgency based on market conditions
Email 5: The "breakup email" that reverses psychology

Requirements:
- Subject lines under 40 characters that create curiosity
- Body copy under 150 words per email
- Clear but soft CTAs
- Spacing recommendation between emails
- Mobile-optimized formatting

Agent name: [YOUR NAME]
Target neighborhood: [AREA]
Recent relevant sale: [ADDRESS AND PRICE]
Current market condition: [BUYER'S/SELLER'S MARKET + STATS]

Expired Listing Follow-Up Scripts That Convert

Expired listings represent one of the highest-converting lead sources in real estate—but only if you approach them correctly. These homeowners are frustrated, skeptical of agents, and have already been burned once. Your outreach needs to acknowledge their situation while positioning yourself as the solution.

Prompt #3: The Empathy-First Expired Listing Script

Copy-Paste Prompt
Create a phone script for contacting expired listing homeowners that prioritizes empathy over selling. The homeowner's listing expired [X days/weeks] ago after being on market for [DAYS ON MARKET].

The script must:
- Acknowledge their frustration WITHOUT badmouthing their previous agent
- Ask diagnostic questions to understand what went wrong
- Subtly differentiate your approach based on common expired listing problems (pricing, marketing, showing feedback)
- Offer a "no-pressure" next step (market analysis, strategy session)
- Include responses to these objections: "I'm taking a break from selling," "I'm interviewing other agents," "The market is just bad right now"

Property details: [BEDS/BATHS/SQFT/LAST LIST PRICE]
Days expired: [NUMBER]
Original days on market: [NUMBER]
Your key differentiator: [e.g., "professional staging included," "video marketing specialist," "sold 3 expired listings in this area last quarter"]

The beauty of this prompt is that it positions you as a problem-solver rather than just another agent trying to get a listing. When you lead with empathy and genuine curiosity about what went wrong, you disarm the defensive posture most expired listing owners have.

Prompt #4: The Expired Listing Direct Mail Letter

Copy-Paste Prompt
Write a direct mail letter for an expired listing homeowner that stands out from the typical agent spam they're receiving. This letter should:

- Use a compelling opening line that creates immediate curiosity
- Reference one specific thing about their property that you noticed (from MLS photos or public records)
- Share a brief, relevant success story without sounding boastful
- Explain your "different approach" in 2-3 concrete points
- Include a handwritten-style P.S. that contains the actual call-to-action

Keep the letter under 300 words. Make it feel personal, not mass-produced.

Property address: [ADDRESS]
Something unique about the property: [e.g., "beautiful backyard garden," "updated kitchen," "corner lot"]
Your name: [NAME]
Contact info: [PHONE/EMAIL]
Relevant success story: [BRIEF DESCRIPTION]

FSBO Conversion Scripts That Build Trust

For Sale By Owner sellers are a unique breed. They've decided they don't need an agent—which means your approach can't be about convincing them they're wrong. Instead, you need to position yourself as a resource who respects their decision while planting seeds about the value you provide.

Prompt #5: The Value-First FSBO Approach

Copy-Paste Prompt
Create a multi-touch FSBO approach sequence (phone script + follow-up materials) that positions the agent as a helpful resource rather than a pushy salesperson.

Initial call script that:
- Congratulates them on taking charge of their sale
- Offers one specific, genuinely helpful tip (not a veiled sales pitch)
- Asks permission to send useful resources
- Doesn't ask for the listing on the first call

Follow-up package contents (describe what to include):
- A "FSBO Success Checklist" outline
- Sample comparable analysis for their area
- Offer of services à la carte (photography, open house hosting, contract review)

Second call script (1 week later) that:
- Checks in on their progress
- Addresses common FSBO challenges naturally
- Offers specific help without being pushy

FSBO property: [ADDRESS]
List price: [PRICE]
Days on market: [DAYS]
Your à la carte services: [LIST SERVICES YOU'D OFFER INDIVIDUALLY]

This approach works because FSBOs often become listings after 3-4 weeks of frustration. By being helpful early—without pressuring them—you become the obvious choice when they're ready to make a change.

Sphere of Influence Messaging That Generates Referrals

Your sphere of influence (SOI) is your most valuable lead generation asset, yet most agents either ignore it or over-communicate with generic newsletters. The sweet spot is consistent, valuable touchpoints that keep you top-of-mind without being annoying.

Prompt #6: The Quarterly SOI Check-In

Copy-Paste Prompt
Create a personal email template for quarterly sphere of influence check-ins that generates referrals without directly asking for them. The email should:

- Feel genuinely personal, not like a mass marketing email
- Include a brief personal update or observation (seasonal, local event, etc.)
- Share one interesting real estate market insight they'd actually care about
- Include a subtle "referral trigger" that reminds them what you do without being salesy
- Have a casual, conversational tone

Create 4 versions (Q1, Q2, Q3, Q4) with seasonal relevance.

Agent name: [NAME]
Market area: [CITY/REGION]
One recent personal highlight: [e.g., "kids started soccer," "tried that new restaurant downtown"]
Current market snapshot: [1-2 SENTENCES ABOUT MARKET CONDITIONS]

Prompt #7: The "Just Sold" SOI Notification

Copy-Paste Prompt
Write a "Just Sold" announcement for my sphere of influence that celebrates the sale while naturally generating referral conversations. This should NOT be a typical "just sold" postcard message.

Instead, create a short story format that:
- Briefly shares the client's journey (challenge they faced, how we solved it)
- Highlights one unexpected win or interesting detail from the transaction
- Includes a genuine expression of gratitude
- Ends with a soft referral request that doesn't feel pushy

Make it feel like sharing good news with a friend, not a marketing announcement.

Property type: [DESCRIBE]
Client situation: [FIRST-TIME BUYER/DOWNSIZING/RELOCATING/ETC.]
Challenge overcome: [BRIEF DESCRIPTION]
Outcome: [SALE PRICE, DAYS ON MARKET, ANY OTHER WIN]
Interesting detail: [SOMETHING MEMORABLE ABOUT THE TRANSACTION]

These SOI prompts are designed to maintain relationships at scale. For more relationship-building strategies, explore our free prompt library with additional templates for client communication.

Social Media Lead Generation Prompts

Social media is where modern real estate lead generation AI really shines. The agents winning on social aren't posting listing photos—they're creating content that attracts, engages, and converts their ideal clients.

Prompt #8: The Social Content Calendar Generator

Copy-Paste Prompt
Create a 30-day social media content calendar for a real estate agent focused on lead generation. Each post should be designed to attract [BUYER/SELLER/BOTH] leads in [MARKET AREA].

Include these content types distributed throughout the month:
- 8 educational posts (market insights, buying/selling tips)
- 6 engagement posts (questions, polls, hot takes)
- 4 social proof posts (testimonials, just sold, client wins)
- 4 personal brand posts (behind-the-scenes, values, personality)
- 4 direct lead generation posts (free offer, consultation, etc.)
- 4 community/local content posts

For each post, provide:
- Platform recommendation (Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn, TikTok)
- Hook/opening line
- Key talking points or full caption
- Call-to-action
- Hashtag suggestions
- Best time to post

Target audience: [DESCRIBE IDEAL CLIENT]
Agent's personality/brand: [DESCRIBE YOUR VIBE]
Unique selling points: [WHAT MAKES YOU DIFFERENT]
Local market hooks: [NEIGHBORHOODS, EVENTS, LOCAL TOPICS]

This comprehensive prompt generates an entire month of strategic content designed to attract leads, not just likes. The key is being specific about your target audience and brand personality so the content feels authentically you.

Maximizing Your Results with These Prompts

Having great prompts is only half the battle. Here's how to get maximum ROI from your ChatGPT lead gen real estate efforts:

Customize religiously. The prompts above are frameworks, not finished products. The more specific detail you add about your market, your clients, and your unique value proposition, the better your outputs will be.

Test and iterate. Track which scripts and messages generate responses. When something works, feed that success back into ChatGPT to generate more variations. When something flops, analyze why and adjust your prompts accordingly.

Maintain your voice. Always review and edit AI-generated content to match your authentic communication style. Your prospects will eventually meet you in person—the messaging needs to match the reality.

Combine with systems. These prompts work best when integrated into a consistent prospecting routine. Schedule your cold calls, automate your email sequences, and batch your social content creation.
