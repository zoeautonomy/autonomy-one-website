Cold Outreach Scripts That Actually Get Responses

Cold outreach remains one of the most effective—and most dreaded—lead generation tactics. The challenge isn't making the calls; it's knowing what to say when someone picks up. These prompts help you craft opening lines and conversation flows that disarm skepticism and create genuine engagement.

Prompt #1: The Neighborhood Value Update Call

This approach positions you as a market expert providing valuable information rather than another agent asking for business. It's particularly effective in areas with significant recent sales activity.

Copy-Paste Prompt: You are an expert real estate copywriter specializing in cold outreach scripts. Write a cold call script for a real estate agent calling homeowners in [NEIGHBORHOOD NAME] to share recent market activity. The script should: 1. Open with a specific, attention-grabbing local market statistic 2. Mention 1-2 recent nearby sales with approximate prices 3. Offer a free, no-obligation home value estimate 4. Handle the objection "I'm not interested in selling right now" 5. Close with a soft ask for a brief 5-minute conversation Tone: Confident but not pushy, neighborly and knowledgeable. Keep the script under 200 words for natural delivery.

Prompt #2: The Circle Prospecting Script

When you've just listed or sold a property, circle prospecting lets you leverage that success into new opportunities. This prompt creates a script that maximizes the social proof of your recent transaction.

Copy-Paste Prompt: Create a circle prospecting phone script for a real estate agent who just [LISTED/SOLD] a home at [ADDRESS]. The script should: 1. Introduce the reason for calling (the recent listing/sale nearby) 2. Share one compelling detail about the transaction (price point, days on market, multiple offers) 3. Naturally transition to asking if they've considered their own home's value 4. Include a response for "We're not thinking about moving" 5. Offer to add them to a monthly neighborhood market update email 6. End with a friendly, low-pressure close Keep the tone conversational and avoid sounding scripted. The agent's name is [YOUR NAME] with [BROKERAGE].

Expired Listing Follow-Up Scripts

Expired listings represent some of the hottest leads in real estate—these are motivated sellers who've already decided to sell. The challenge is standing out from the dozen other agents calling them the moment their listing expires. These prompts help you create approaches that differentiate you from the competition.

Prompt #3: The Empathetic Expired Listing Approach

Most agents call expired listings with aggressive tactics. This prompt creates a script that acknowledges the seller's frustration and positions you as a problem-solver rather than another salesperson.

Copy-Paste Prompt: Write an empathetic cold call script for contacting homeowners whose listing just expired. The script should: 1. Acknowledge their frustration without criticizing their previous agent 2. Ask one diagnostic question about their experience (e.g., "What do you feel was the biggest challenge?") 3. Briefly mention 2-3 specific strategies you use that might have made a difference (without being arrogant) 4. Offer a free, no-obligation market analysis with a specific insight they likely haven't seen 5. Handle the objection "I'm taking a break from selling" 6. Close with a specific next step (not a generic "call me if you change your mind") The tone should be empathetic, confident, and solution-oriented. Avoid any language that sounds like you're criticizing their previous agent directly.

Prompt #4: The Expired Listing Email Sequence

Sometimes homeowners with expired listings need time to decompress before re-engaging. This prompt creates a nurture sequence that stays top-of-mind without being pushy.

Copy-Paste Prompt: Create a 3-email nurture sequence for homeowners whose listing expired [X WEEKS AGO]. Each email should: Email 1 (sent immediately): Acknowledge the situation, offer genuine value (market insight), soft call-to-action Email 2 (sent 5 days later): Share a brief success story of selling a similar "difficult" property, medium call-to-action Email 3 (sent 10 days later): Present a specific, time-sensitive opportunity or market shift, stronger call-to-action Requirements: - Each email under 150 words - Subject lines that avoid spam triggers and create curiosity - Personal, human tone (not corporate) - Clear differentiation from typical agent emails - Include the property address [ADDRESS] naturally Agent name: [YOUR NAME], specializing in [AREA/PROPERTY TYPE]

FSBO Scripts That Convert Skeptical Sellers

For Sale By Owner leads require a unique approach. These sellers have actively chosen not to work with agents, which means traditional pitches fall flat. The key is demonstrating value without triggering their defenses. Real estate lead generation AI excels at crafting these nuanced conversations.

Prompt #5: The FSBO Partnership Approach

Instead of trying to convince FSBOs they're making a mistake, this script positions you as a potential resource—even if they don't list with you.

Copy-Paste Prompt: Write a FSBO contact script that positions the agent as a helpful resource rather than a pushy salesperson. The script should: 1. Compliment something specific about their listing (based on [LISTING DETAILS/PRICE/PHOTOS]) 2. Acknowledge and respect their decision to sell independently 3. Offer ONE specific, immediately valuable piece of information (comparable sales data, buyer traffic trends, or pricing insight) 4. Ask permission to send helpful resources with no strings attached 5. Plant the seed that you're available if they decide they want professional support 6. Handle the objection "I don't want to pay commission" 7. Close with an offer to preview the home and provide honest feedback Tone: Respectful, genuinely helpful, zero pressure. The goal is to start a relationship, not close a listing appointment on the first call.

Prompt #6: The FSBO Follow-Up After 30 Days

Many FSBOs become more receptive after experiencing the challenges of selling independently. This prompt creates a perfectly-timed follow-up that capitalizes on that shift.

Copy-Paste Prompt: Create a follow-up script for contacting a FSBO seller whose property has been on the market for 30+ days without selling. The script should: 1. Reference your previous conversation/contact casually 2. Ask how the selling process has been going (open-ended, curious) 3. Share ONE market insight relevant to their situation (days on market trends, buyer behavior shifts) 4. Gently introduce the idea that professional marketing might increase their exposure 5. Offer a specific service or analysis (not a full listing pitch) 6. Handle "I'm going to keep trying on my own for a while" 7. Leave the door open for future contact The property is at [ADDRESS], listed at [PRICE], and has been on market for [X DAYS]. Be empathetic but also subtly highlight the cost of extended days on market.

Sphere of Influence Messages That Spark Referrals

Your sphere of influence—past clients, friends, family, and professional connections—represents your most valuable lead source. Yet most agents either ignore their sphere or blast them with generic newsletters that get deleted immediately. ChatGPT lead gen real estate prompts can help you create personalized touches that keep you top-of-mind.

Prompt #7: The Personal Check-In Message

This prompt creates messages that feel genuinely personal while subtly reminding your sphere that you're active in real estate.

Copy-Paste Prompt: Write 5 different personalized check-in messages for a real estate agent to send to past clients and sphere of influence contacts. Each message should: 1. Lead with something personal (not real estate related) 2. Include a natural transition to a real estate conversation starter 3. NOT directly ask for referrals (too pushy) 4. Include a soft "plant the seed" mention of your real estate activity 5. End with a genuine question that invites response Create variations for: - A past buyer client (purchased 2 years ago) - A past seller client (sold 6 months ago) - A friend who hasn't bought/sold but might know someone - A professional contact (lender, attorney, etc.) - A family member Tone: Warm, personal, NOT salesy. These should feel like messages from a friend who happens to sell real estate, not a real estate agent fishing for business.

Social Media Lead Generation Prompts

Social media offers unprecedented access to potential clients, but only if your content stands out. These prompts help you create scroll-stopping content that generates inbound leads.

Prompt #8: The Local Market Hot Take Post

Positioning yourself as a market expert requires sharing insights others aren't talking about. This prompt helps you create thought-provoking content that drives engagement and DMs.

Copy-Paste Prompt: Create a social media post (for Instagram/Facebook) that shares a "hot take" or contrarian insight about the current real estate market in [YOUR CITY/AREA]. The post should: 1. Open with a bold, attention-grabbing statement or statistic 2. Explain the insight in 2-3 accessible sentences (avoid jargon) 3. Connect it to what this means for buyers OR sellers specifically 4. End with a question that encourages comments/engagement 5. Include a subtle call-to-action (DM me for more details, link in bio, etc.) Requirements: - Under 150 words (optimized for social attention spans) - Use line breaks for readability - Include 3-5 relevant hashtags - Suggest an emoji placement that enhances but doesn't overwhelm Market data to incorporate: [INSERT ANY RECENT STATS OR TRENDS YOU'VE NOTICED]

Best Practices for Using ChatGPT Lead Generation Prompts

Having the right prompts is only half the equation. How you use them determines your results. Here are the principles that separate agents who see ROI from those who abandon AI tools within a week.

Always Customize the Output

ChatGPT gives you a strong first draft, but your local knowledge and personality must shine through. Add neighborhood-specific details, reference local landmarks, and inject your natural speaking rhythm. The goal is scripts that sound like you on your best day, not a robot reading marketing copy.

Test Different Approaches

Use ChatGPT to generate multiple versions of the same script, then A/B test them in real conversations. Track which openings get the best responses, which objection handlers actually work, and which closes lead to appointments. This data-driven approach will refine your scripts over time.

Layer in Compliance Awareness

Always review AI-generated content for compliance with fair housing laws, your brokerage's policies, and local regulations. ChatGPT doesn't automatically know the legal constraints in your market, so you must be the final compliance checkpoint.

Build a Personal Prompt Library

As you discover which prompts work best for your style and market, save them in a document you can reference quickly. Over time, you'll build a personalized prompt library that becomes one of your most valuable business assets.
