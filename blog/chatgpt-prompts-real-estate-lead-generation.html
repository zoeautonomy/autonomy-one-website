Cold Outreach Scripts That Get Responses

Cold outreach is notoriously difficult because you're interrupting someone's day with no prior relationship. The key is to lead with value and make your message feel relevant to their specific situation. Generic "I'm an agent in your area" messages get deleted. Targeted, thoughtful outreach gets meetings.

Prompt #1: Neighborhood-Specific Cold Email

Copy-Paste Prompt: Act as an experienced real estate copywriter. Write a cold email to homeowners in [NEIGHBORHOOD NAME] who might be considering selling in the next 6-12 months. The email should: - Open with a specific, compelling fact about recent sales in their neighborhood - Mention that I recently sold [ADDRESS OR "a home on their street"] for [RESULT - above asking, in X days, etc.] - Position me as a neighborhood specialist without being salesy - Include a soft call to action offering a no-obligation home value estimate - Keep it under 150 words and conversational in tone My name is [YOUR NAME] and I work with [BROKERAGE]. My unique value prop is [YOUR DIFFERENTIATOR].

This prompt works because it focuses on hyper-local relevance. When someone sees that you just sold a home on their street, you're no longer a random agent—you're the neighborhood expert. The key is filling in those brackets with real, specific information that demonstrates your activity and success in their area.

Prompt #2: Cold Calling Script with Objection Paths

Copy-Paste Prompt: Create a cold calling script for reaching homeowners in [NEIGHBORHOOD/ZIP CODE]. The script should: 1. Have a pattern-interrupt opening that doesn't sound like a typical sales call 2. Quickly establish relevance by mentioning local market activity 3. Include a qualifying question to determine timeline 4. Have 3 branching paths based on their response: "interested now," "maybe in 6+ months," or "not interested" 5. For each path, provide a natural response that keeps the conversation going or ends gracefully 6. Include specific rebuttals for "I already have an agent" and "I'm not selling" Make the tone confident but not pushy. The goal is to start a relationship, not pressure for a listing appointment on the first call.

Cold calling still works when done right. The difference between scripts that convert and scripts that get hang-ups often comes down to that first 10 seconds. This prompt helps you create openings that break the pattern of typical sales calls while still moving toward your objective.

Expired Listing Follow-Up Scripts

Expired listings represent some of the warmest leads in real estate—these are motivated sellers who've already decided to sell but experienced failure with another agent. The challenge is that every agent in town is calling them. Your outreach needs to stand out while addressing the elephant in the room: their listing didn't sell.

Prompt #3: Expired Listing Initial Contact

Copy-Paste Prompt: Write a voicemail script and follow-up text message for an expired listing at [ADDRESS]. Research context: the home was listed for [DAYS ON MARKET] days at [PRICE], and comparable homes in the area are selling for [COMP PRICE RANGE]. The voicemail should: - Be under 30 seconds - Acknowledge their frustration without badmouthing the previous agent - Offer ONE specific insight about why their home may not have sold - Create curiosity without giving away everything The text message should: - Reference the voicemail - Include a simple yes/no question - Feel personal, not templated Avoid phrases like "I'm sorry your home didn't sell" or "I can do better." Focus on value and specific market insights.

The psychology here is important. Expired listing sellers are frustrated and often skeptical of agents. Leading with empathy while demonstrating specific market knowledge positions you differently than agents who just promise to "try harder."

Prompt #4: Expired Listing Differentiation Email

Copy-Paste Prompt: Create an email for an expired listing homeowner that differentiates me from the 20+ other agents contacting them. The email should: - Acknowledge that they're being bombarded by agents right now - NOT promise that I'll sell their home (everyone says that) - Instead, offer to show them the 3 specific reasons homes in their price range aren't selling right now in [MARKET] - Include a brief case study of how I helped a similar seller in [NEIGHBORHOOD] who was in the same situation - End with a low-commitment offer (15-minute call, not a full listing presentation) Tone: Confident, direct, no fluff. Make it clear I'm not desperate for their listing.

This approach works because it flips the typical expired listing script on its head. Instead of begging for the listing, you're positioning yourself as a consultant who can diagnose what went wrong. That's a much more attractive value proposition to a frustrated seller.

FSBO Scripts That Build Trust

For Sale By Owner prospects present a unique challenge: they've explicitly decided they don't want to work with an agent. Any outreach that sounds like a traditional listing pitch will be immediately rejected. The key is to position yourself as a resource, not a salesperson.

Prompt #5: FSBO Value-First Approach

Copy-Paste Prompt: Write a door-knocking script for approaching FSBO sellers. The script should: - Open by complimenting something specific about their home or marketing (yard sign, online photos, etc.) - NOT immediately ask if they'd consider listing with an agent - Instead, offer one piece of free value: a list of buyer's agents who are actively looking for homes in their price range - Explain how connecting with these agents could help them sell faster even without listing - Plant a seed about "if things change" without being pushy - Include a graceful exit if they're not interested The goal is to become a trusted resource they think of if their FSBO attempt doesn't work out.

Statistics show that the majority of FSBOs eventually list with an agent. By being the helpful professional who offered value without pressure, you become the natural choice when they're ready. This long-game approach converts at higher rates than aggressive pitches.

Sphere of Influence Messages

Your sphere of influence—past clients, friends, family, and acquaintances—represents your most valuable lead source. Yet most agents either neglect their sphere entirely or reach out only when they need something. Real estate lead generation AI can help you stay top-of-mind with consistent, genuine touchpoints.

Prompt #6: Quarterly Sphere Check-In

Copy-Paste Prompt: Write 4 different quarterly check-in messages for my sphere of influence (past clients and personal contacts). Each message should: - Feel personal and not like a mass email - NOT directly ask for referrals (that's too obvious) - Include something of genuine value: market update, home maintenance tip, local event, or neighborhood news - Naturally remind them what I do without being salesy - End with a conversation-starter question Create versions for: Q1 (new year), Q2 (spring market), Q3 (summer), Q4 (holiday season). Make each one feel seasonally appropriate and timely.

The best sphere marketing doesn't feel like marketing at all. These touchpoints should genuinely add value to your contacts' lives while keeping you top-of-mind when they hear someone mention buying or selling.

Prompt #7: Past Client Anniversary Message

Copy-Paste Prompt: Create a "home anniversary" email template for past clients on the 1-year, 3-year, and 5-year anniversary of their purchase. Each message should: - Celebrate their milestone with genuine warmth - Include a brief update on how their home's value has changed (template: "Homes in [NEIGHBORHOOD] have appreciated approximately X% since you purchased") - Offer something useful: home maintenance reminder, refinance consideration, or neighborhood update - Subtly ask if they know anyone looking to experience the same joy of homeownership - Feel celebratory, not transactional Make each anniversary message slightly different in tone and content.

Home anniversaries are powerful touchpoints because they're genuinely meaningful to homeowners. This isn't manufactured urgency—it's a real milestone worth celebrating, and it creates a natural opportunity to reconnect.

Social Media Lead Generation Prompts

Social media has become a primary lead generation channel for real estate agents, but most agents struggle to create content that generates actual leads rather than just likes. The key is creating content that attracts potential buyers and sellers while positioning you as the go-to expert in your market.

Prompt #8: Social Media Content Calendar

Copy-Paste Prompt: Create a 2-week social media content calendar for a real estate agent focused on lead generation. For each day, provide: 1. Content type (carousel, video script, single image post, story idea) 2. Specific topic with hook 3. Caption with call-to-action 4. Relevant hashtags The content should balance: - Educational posts that establish expertise (40%) - Local market updates that attract sellers (25%) - Lifestyle/community content that attracts buyers (25%) - Direct lead generation posts with clear CTAs (10%) Market: [YOUR CITY/AREA] Target audience: [FIRST-TIME BUYERS / MOVE-UP SELLERS / DOWNSIZERS / ETC.] Make the hooks scroll-stopping and the CTAs low-friction (DM me, comment below, link in bio).

Social media lead generation works best when you're consistently present with valuable content. This prompt helps you plan ahead so you're not scrambling for post ideas daily. For more social media strategies and prompts, check out our free prompts library.

Making These Prompts Work for You

The prompts above are starting points, not final scripts. The agents who see the best results from ChatGPT prompts for real estate lead generation customize them extensively for their market, personality, and brand voice. Here are tips for maximizing their effectiveness:

  • Add specific details: Replace every bracket with real information. Specificity is what makes AI-generated content feel personal.
  • Test and iterate: Use A/B testing on your emails and messages. Let data tell you what resonates with your market.
  • Maintain your voice: Read every AI output aloud. If it doesn't sound like you, edit until it does.
  • Combine channels: The best lead generation campaigns use multiple touchpoints—email, phone, text, and social working together.
  • Track everything: Know which prompts and scripts generate the most conversations and, ultimately, closings.
