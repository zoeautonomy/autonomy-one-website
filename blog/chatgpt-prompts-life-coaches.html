Session Preparation Prompts: Show Up More Prepared Than Ever

Great coaching sessions don't happen by accident. They require intentional preparation that honors where your client is on their journey while strategically moving them toward their goals. Here's how ChatGPT can help you prepare more effectively.

Prompt #1: Pre-Session Review and Strategy

Session Prep Prompt: I'm a life coach preparing for a session with a client. Here's their background: [INSERT CLIENT DETAILS - goals, challenges, where they are in your program]. Our last session covered [BRIEF SUMMARY]. They mentioned struggling with [SPECIFIC CHALLENGE]. Generate a strategic session outline that includes: 1) A powerful opening question to check in on their progress, 2) Three coaching angles I could explore based on their challenge, 3) A potential breakthrough question that could shift their perspective, and 4) A meaningful action item framework for accountability. Keep the tone warm but direct.

This prompt transforms your scattered notes into a cohesive session strategy. The key is providing enough context about your client (while respecting confidentiality by avoiding identifying details) so ChatGPT can generate truly relevant suggestions.

Prompt #2: Customized Reflection Questions

Reflection Questions Prompt: Create 5 deep reflection questions for a coaching client who is working on [SPECIFIC GOAL, e.g., "building confidence to ask for a promotion"]. They tend to be [PERSONALITY TRAIT, e.g., "analytical and sometimes stuck in overthinking"]. The questions should bypass surface-level answers and help them access deeper wisdom. Include a mix of past-reflection, present-awareness, and future-visioning questions.

Client Discovery Questions: Nail Your Intake Process

The discovery call is where coaching relationships are won or lost. You need questions that quickly uncover whether someone is a good fit while also demonstrating your expertise and building trust. Here's a prompt that helps you create discovery frameworks for any niche.

Prompt #3: Discovery Call Question Framework

Discovery Call Prompt: I'm a life coach specializing in [YOUR NICHE, e.g., "career transitions for mid-career professionals"]. Create a discovery call question framework with 8-10 questions that accomplish the following: 1) Quickly identify their core pain points and desires, 2) Uncover their readiness for change and coachability, 3) Help them articulate what success would look like, 4) Naturally position my coaching as the solution without being salesy. Format each question with a brief note on what information it reveals. End with a suggested transition into discussing my program.

This prompt is particularly valuable when you're expanding into a new coaching niche or refining your intake process. The "what information it reveals" component helps you listen more strategically during actual calls.

Prompt #4: Client Intake Questionnaire

Intake Questionnaire Prompt: Design a comprehensive client intake questionnaire for my life coaching practice focused on [YOUR SPECIALTY]. Include sections for: personal background, current life satisfaction assessment (with rating scales), goal clarification, past experience with coaching or personal development, potential obstacles they anticipate, their preferred communication and accountability styles, and commitment level assessment. Make questions specific enough to gather useful information but open-ended enough to reveal how they think and communicate. Keep it under 20 questions total.

Social Media Content: Attract Your Ideal Clients Consistently

Content creation is often the biggest time drain for life coaches. You know you need to show up consistently on social media, but coming up with fresh, engaging content while running a practice feels impossible. These prompts will help you batch-create weeks of content in hours.

Prompt #5: Instagram Carousel Content

Instagram Carousel Prompt: Create an Instagram carousel post (7 slides) for life coaches targeting [YOUR IDEAL CLIENT, e.g., "ambitious women in their 30s feeling unfulfilled in their corporate careers"]. Topic: [TOPIC, e.g., "Signs you're ready for a major life change"]. Slide 1 should be a scroll-stopping hook. Slides 2-6 should deliver value with punchy, relatable points. Slide 7 should include a soft CTA that invites engagement. Keep each slide under 30 words. Include emoji suggestions and relevant hashtags. Match the tone of [DESCRIBE YOUR BRAND VOICE, e.g., "empowering but warm, like a supportive best friend who also pushes you"].

Prompt #6: LinkedIn Thought Leadership Post

LinkedIn Post Prompt: Write a LinkedIn post from the perspective of a life coach that shares a counterintuitive insight about [TOPIC, e.g., "why setting big goals might be holding you back"]. Start with a provocative hook that challenges conventional wisdom. Share a brief client transformation story (anonymized) that illustrates the point. End with a question that sparks discussion. Keep it under 200 words. The tone should be confident, slightly contrarian, but ultimately helpful. Include a P.S. with a subtle mention of my coaching work.

Program Marketing Copy: Fill Your Programs Without the Struggle

Whether you're launching a group program, selling a course, or promoting your 1:1 packages, compelling marketing copy is essential. These ChatGPT life coach scripts help you articulate your value in words that resonate with your ideal clients.

Prompt #7: Sales Page Section Generator

Sales Page Prompt: Write the "Is This For You?" section of a sales page for my [PROGRAM TYPE, e.g., "12-week group coaching program"] called [PROGRAM NAME]. My ideal clients are [DETAILED DESCRIPTION]. Create two lists: "This is for you if..." (8 points describing who will benefit most) and "This is NOT for you if..." (5 points that filter out poor-fit clients). The "for you" points should feel like I'm reading their mind about their struggles and desires. The "not for you" points should actually make ideal clients feel MORE confident they belong. Use second person throughout and make it emotionally resonant without being manipulative.

Prompt #8: Email Sequence for Program Launch

Email Sequence Prompt: Create a 5-email launch sequence for my life coaching program [PROGRAM NAME]. The program helps [WHO] achieve [WHAT RESULT] through [YOUR METHOD/APPROACH]. Email 1: Story-based introduction that builds connection and hints at transformation. Email 2: Address the #1 objection my audience has: [OBJECTION]. Email 3: Social proof email featuring client transformation themes. Email 4: The "what makes this different" email explaining my unique approach. Email 5: Cart-close email with urgency and final invitation. For each email, include subject line options, preview text, and the full body. Keep emails scannable with clear formatting.

Strong email copy is the backbone of successful launches. Learn more strategies in our article on email marketing prompts for small businesses.

Testimonial Request Scripts: Gather Social Proof Effortlessly

Testimonials are gold for life coaches, but asking for them feels awkward, and clients often don't know what to say. These prompts solve both problems.

Prompt #9: Testimonial Request Email

Testimonial Request Prompt: Write a warm email requesting a testimonial from a coaching client who just completed [PROGRAM/PACKAGE]. The email should: 1) Celebrate their specific transformation or achievements, 2) Explain why testimonials matter for helping others like them find coaching, 3) Make it easy by providing 4-5 specific questions they can answer (questions that will naturally produce compelling testimonials), 4) Offer options for written, video, or quick voice memo, 5) Include a gentle mention that I can write a draft based on their answers if writing isn't their strength. Keep it genuinely appreciative, not transactional.

Prompt #10: Testimonial Polishing

Testimonial Polish Prompt: Here's a raw testimonial from a coaching client: [PASTE TESTIMONIAL]. Help me polish it for my website while keeping it authentic to their voice. Make it more concise (aim for 75-100 words), ensure it highlights specific results or transformations, and structure it with the problem-solution-result flow. Create two versions: one for a long-form testimonial section and one as a short pull-quote for social media. Flag any parts that might need client approval for the edits.

Best Practices for Using ChatGPT as a Life Coach

Before you dive in with these prompts, here are some important guidelines to maximize your results while maintaining the integrity of your practice:

1. Always Personalize the Output

ChatGPT gives you an excellent starting point, but your unique voice, methodology, and understanding of your specific clients should always be woven in. Use AI-generated content as a foundation, not a final product.

2. Protect Client Confidentiality

When using prompts related to session prep or client work, never include identifying information. Use general descriptions ("a client in their 40s navigating a divorce") rather than names or specific details. Your clients' privacy must remain sacred.

3. Use AI to Enhance, Not Replace, Your Intuition

ChatGPT can suggest powerful questions or session strategies, but your intuition in the moment with a client is irreplaceable. Let AI inform your preparation while remaining fully present and responsive in sessions.

4. Batch Your AI Work

Rather than using ChatGPT reactively throughout the day, schedule dedicated AI-assisted work blocks. Batch-create a month of social content, draft all your email sequences at once, or prepare session outlines for the entire week. This protects your creative flow and coaching energy.

5. Iterate and Save Your Best Prompts

When you find prompts that consistently produce great results, save them in a swipe file. Small tweaks to prompts can dramatically improve outputs, so track what works best for your voice and niche.
