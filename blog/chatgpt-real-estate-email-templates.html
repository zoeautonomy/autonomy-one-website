Template 1: New Lead Welcome Email

The first touchpoint with a new lead sets the tone for your entire relationship. This email should be warm, professional, and action-oriented without overwhelming the recipient.

The Prompt

Write a warm, professional welcome email from a real estate agent to a new lead who just signed up on their website. The email should: introduce the agent briefly, acknowledge what the lead is looking for (buying/selling), offer immediate value, and include a clear call-to-action for scheduling a call. Keep it under 150 words. Tone: friendly and helpful, not salesy.

The Generated Email

Subject Line

Welcome! Let's Find Your Perfect Home Together

Email Body

Hi [First Name],

Thank you for reaching out—I'm excited to help you on your home buying journey!

I'm [Agent Name], a local real estate specialist with [X] years of experience in [Area]. I've helped hundreds of families find homes they love, and I'd be honored to do the same for you.

Based on your inquiry, it looks like you're interested in [property type/area]. I've attached a quick market snapshot with current listings that match your criteria.

I'd love to learn more about what you're looking for. Would you have 15 minutes this week for a quick call? You can book a time directly here: [Calendar Link]

Looking forward to connecting!

Warm regards,
[Agent Name]
[Phone] | [Email]

Template 2: Buyer Consultation Follow-Up

After meeting with a potential buyer, prompt follow-up reinforces your professionalism and keeps momentum going. This ChatGPT follow up email real estate template ensures you stay top of mind.

The Prompt

Write a follow-up email from a real estate agent to a buyer they just had a consultation with. Recap the key points discussed (budget range, preferred neighborhoods, timeline), outline next steps, and express enthusiasm about working together. Professional but personable tone. Under 200 words.

The Generated Email

Subject Line

Great Meeting You Today – Here's Our Game Plan

Email Body

Hi [First Name],

It was wonderful meeting with you today! I'm genuinely excited to help you find your perfect home.

Here's a quick recap of what we discussed:

  • Budget range: $[X] - $[Y]
  • Preferred areas: [Neighborhoods]
  • Must-haves: [Key features]
  • Timeline: Looking to close by [Date]

Next Steps:

  1. I'll set up a custom search alert based on your criteria
  2. Connect you with my preferred lender for pre-approval (if needed)
  3. Schedule our first round of showings for [Day/Date]

I'll send over some listings by tomorrow that I think you'll love. In the meantime, feel free to reach out with any questions—I'm always just a text away.

Let's find you that dream home!

Best,
[Agent Name]

Template 3: Seller Price Reduction Recommendation

One of the most delicate conversations in real estate is recommending a price reduction. This email approaches the topic with data and empathy, making the difficult conversation easier.

The Prompt

Write a tactful email from a real estate agent to a seller recommending a price reduction on their listing. Include market data justification, acknowledge their feelings, explain the benefits of adjusting price now versus waiting, and propose a specific action. Empathetic and professional tone. Under 250 words.

The Generated Email

Subject Line

Market Update on [Property Address] – Let's Discuss Strategy

Email Body

Hi [First Name],

I wanted to share some important market insights about your listing at [Address] and discuss a strategy adjustment that could help us attract more qualified buyers.

Here's what the data shows:

  • Your home has been on market for [X] days
  • We've had [X] showings with [X] follow-up inquiries
  • Similar homes in your area are selling at $[Average Price]
  • Buyer feedback consistently mentions price as the hesitation point

I know this isn't the news anyone wants to hear, and I understand how much your home means to you. The good news is that we're seeing strong buyer activity in your area—they just need a compelling reason to act.

My recommendation: A strategic adjustment to $[New Price] would position us competitively against current inventory and likely generate multiple offers within the first two weeks.

The longer we wait, the more buyers assume something is wrong with the property. Acting now protects your equity and timeline.

Can we schedule a call tomorrow to discuss? I have availability at [Time] or [Time].

I'm committed to getting you the best possible outcome.

Sincerely,
[Agent Name]

  • Share my contact: [Phone] | [Email]

Thank you for trusting me with such an important decision. It truly means the world.

Gratefully,
[Agent Name]

Template 8: Re-Engagement Email for Cold Leads

Leads that went silent aren't necessarily lost. This ChatGPT follow up email real estate template re-opens the conversation without being pushy.

The Prompt

Write a re-engagement email from a real estate agent to a lead who went cold 3-6 months ago. Be friendly, not guilt-inducing. Acknowledge time has passed, offer fresh value, and make it easy to re-engage or opt out. Casual and understanding tone. Under 125 words.

The Generated Email

Subject Line

Still Thinking About [Buying/Selling]?

Email Body

Hi [First Name],

I hope this finds you well! We chatted a few months back about your real estate plans, and I wanted to check in.

A lot has changed in the market since then—[brief market update]. If you're still thinking about making a move, I'd love to reconnect and share what's new.

No pressure at all. Just reply with:

  • "Still interested" – I'll send updated options
  • "Not right now" – I'll check back in a few months
  • "Please stop emailing" – No hard feelings, I'll remove you

Either way, I'm here when you need me!

Best,
[Agent Name]
